The core + its seven layers

The top row is FINDINGS — the settled, measured facts. Each row below it holds one epistemic act that FINDINGS.md (measured-fact-only) structurally can't. ⚙️ auto rows were emitted by a seed script and are candidates until a human confirms; ✍️ human rows were written by a session. Rows graduate into FINDINGS / CROSS_REPO_DECISIONS as they mature.

STALENESS — confidence decay

The fully-auto-generated layer. Flag = 🔴 >6 mo since last re-verify · 🟡 3–6 mo · 🟢 fresh (<3 mo) · ⬜ undated.

VERIFIABILITY — how each finding can be re-checked

The re-derivability class of every FINDINGS finding (H1362 ruling). A auto-reproducible (committed script + data) · B re-probeable (live external host) · C historically fixed (dated correspondence / print / standing fact) · D not reproducible as stated — a figure or ledger never committed in recoverable form. Staleness asks when a fact was last checked; this asks whether it can be.

Origin mix — how much is machine-seeded vs human-written

The registries are designed to be seeded automatically and confirmed by hand. A healthy layer trends from ⚙️ toward ✍️ over time as candidates are confirmed or deleted.

Conclusions

    FINDINGS is the core; the seven layers orbit it. A fact starts as an assumption, a gap, or a contradiction; a recipe says how to settle it; once settled it graduates into FINDINGS (or a decision); a failed attempt is parked as a dead end; staleness tells you when a settled FINDINGS fact needs re-checking; and the glossary is the shared vocabulary they are all written in. That is why FINDINGS sits at the top here, not on a separate page. The exact confirm → promote → delete algorithm is PROTOCOL.md.
