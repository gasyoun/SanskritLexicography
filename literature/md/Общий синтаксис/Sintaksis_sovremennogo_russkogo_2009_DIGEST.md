## What the book is

*Синтаксис современного русского языка: Словарь-справочник* ("Syntax of modern Russian: A
dictionary-reference") is a **173-term** teaching-aid dictionary, published as part of a
St. Petersburg State University (SPbGU) educational-materials series, "Русский мир:
учебники для высшей школы" ("Russian World: textbooks for higher education"), funded by a
grant from the "Русский мир" ("Russian World") Foundation. It is explicitly a **companion
volume**, not a standalone reference: the authors' preface states it is one part of a larger
teaching-methods complex — textbook, syllabus/methodological recommendations, exercise
collection, and reader — built around the textbook *Синтаксис современного русского
языка* by G. N. Akimova, S. V. Vyatkina, V. P. Kazakov, and D. V. Rudnev (SPb., 2009). The
term list was deliberately scoped to that textbook's own terminological index (overlapping
but not identical to it) rather than aiming at exhaustive coverage of Russian syntactic
terminology; entries follow a mostly non-inverted alphabetical order for multi-word terms
(e.g. "КОММУНИКАТИВНО-СИЛЬНОЕ ПРЕДЛОЖЕНИЕ," not "предложение коммуникативно-сильное").
The preface notes the authors consulted, as prior art in the same emerging genre of
"syntactic dictionary-reference," A. M. Lomov's *Словарь-справочник по синтаксису
современного русского языка* (M., 2007) and P. A. Lekant et al.'s *Краткий справочник по
современному русскому языку* (1991/1995) — the Lomov title cited here is distinct from the
2004 Voronezh dictionary digested separately in this folder (see
`Lomov_russkii_sintaksis_DIGEST.md`).

**Authorship of entries is individually attributed**, unlike a typical encyclopedia:
each entry closes with initials identifying its author — Г. А. (Галина Николаевна
Акимова, Dr. habil., professor), С. В. (Светлана Вадимовна Вяткина, Candidate of
Philology, associate professor), Д. Р. (Дмитрий Владимирович Руднев, Candidate of
Philology, associate professor), Е. З. (Екатерина Сергеевна Зорина), Е. К. (Екатерина
Сергеевна Кузнецова), В. К. (Василий Викторович Клепацкий) — all faculty/graduate
students of the Russian Language Department at SPbGU's Faculty of Philology and Arts,
working within the syntactic-description tradition established there by Prof. Akimova.

**Sampled scope (front matter's own term list + entries sampled across the alphabet):** the
book's printed term list (reproduced in the front matter, page 6–7 of the conversion) runs
from актант ("actant") and актуальное членение предложения (theme-rheme/functional
sentence perspective) through core clause-structure terms (подлежащее, сказуемое,
словосочетание, сложноподчиненное/сложносочиненное предложение) to text-level and
expressive-syntax terminology (парцелляция, сегментация, экспрессивное
словорасположение, ССЦ/сложное синтаксическое целое) and closes with я-initial entries
on nuclear semantic structure (ядерная семантическая структура). The authors' own
preface states the bulk of entries address the **formal-constructive aspect** of syntax
(sentence/phrase structure analysis), with communicative- and semantic-syntax terms making
up a deliberately smaller share. Each entry gives a definition, etymological/usage notes
where relevant, a historical note on the term's scholarly development, extensive
cross-references to other entries, and illustrative examples drawn from classic and
contemporary literature and periodicals — with competing definitions from different
syntactic schools presented side by side where the term is genuinely contested (e.g. the
entry on член предложения contrasts N. I. Grech's original 19th-century account, the
formal/morphological approach of Potebnya and Peshkovsky, A. A. Shakhmatov's
relations-based classification, and the structural approach of Russian Grammar-80).
The back matter includes a short "Справочная литература" (reference-literature)
bibliography of the standard Russian academic grammars and dictionaries the entries draw
on (АГ-54, АГ-70, РГ-80, РГ-90, Zolotova's 1988/2001 syntactic dictionary, Rozental's
reference grammar, among others).

## Why this book, for this project

Per this repo's own project index, this dictionary's primary use is as a **modern Russian
syntax handbook providing target-language grammatical terminology for translation
quality** — consulted by the RussianTranslation (RuTrans) subproject alongside the Lomov
2004 dictionary digested in this same folder, when a Russian syntactic term used in
`mw_ru`/`pwg_ru` translation work needs an authoritative, individually-attributed,
school-of-thought-aware gloss. It is not a source of Sanskrit- or Indology-specific
content — the book contains none — but a general Russian-metalanguage terminology anchor
for translation quality, comparable in role to the other general Russian-linguistics
reference works digested in this same folder.
