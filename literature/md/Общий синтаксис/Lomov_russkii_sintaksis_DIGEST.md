## What the book is

*Русский синтаксис в алфавитном порядке: Понятийный словарь-справочник* ("Russian syntax
in alphabetical order: A conceptual dictionary-reference") is a single-author terminological
dictionary of Russian syntactic theory, comprising **more than 900 entries** (per the
publisher's own back-cover/copyright-page annotation) covering the core concepts used in
contemporary ("наших дней," i.e. early-2000s) Russian syntactic scholarship. The author's
foreword ("От составителя") states its specific purpose: unlike ordinary textbooks, which
present only the concepts and interpretations consistent with their author's own theoretical
framework and mention competing interpretations only in passing (or in fine print), this
dictionary deliberately surfaces **multiple competing scholarly interpretations of the same
term**, organized to let the reader trace the historical/logical development of research
thinking on each concept — explicitly framed as a corrective to the false impression that
the science of the Russian language is a finished, harmonious system rather than an
unfinished, contested field. Entry headwords are printed as accented (stressed)
small-caps/bold, illustrative material and cited linguistic forms are italicized, and
cross-references to other entries are set in bold lowercase; abbreviations are avoided
except conventional ones.

**Sampled scope (front matter + entries sampled across the alphabet, А through Я):**
entries run from general syntactic-unit and discourse concepts (абзац "paragraph,"
абсолютивное употребление слов) through core semantic-syntax terminology (агенс/агент/
агентив "agent," актант "actant," актуальное членение предложения "functional sentence
perspective"/theme-rheme, предикат) to descriptive/formal syntax (безличное предложение
"impersonal sentence," подчинительная связь, словосочетание) and terminates with
Я-initial entries on nuclear sentence structure (ядерное предложение, ядро предложения).
Entries vary from short cross-reference stubs ("То же, что агенс") to multi-page treatments
citing named scholars and their competing analyses (e.g. the ~900-word entry on
актуальное членение предложения surveys Mathesius's coinage of the term and four
distinct later interpretive traditions — Krushelnitskaya/Kovtunova, Firbas/Daneš,
Shcherba/Vinogradov, Raspopov). The dictionary is explicitly aimed "in the first instance"
at philologists — researchers, university and school teachers, and students — with the
author noting it may also interest a broader circle of readers curious about the scholarly
study of their native language, schoolchildren included.

**Reviewers named on the copyright page:** Z. D. Popova (Doctor of Philology, Professor)
and G. A. Volokhina (Candidate of Philology, Associate Professor).

not two printings of the same book; this digest makes no claim about the 2007 title, which
is not itself held in this repo's `literature/md` folder as far as this review could
determine.

## Why this book, for this project

Per this repo's own project index, this dictionary's primary use is as a **Russian syntax
reference dictionary, organized A–Z, for terminology cross-checking** — consulted by the
RussianTranslation (RuTrans) subproject when translating Petersburg/Grassmann-style
dictionary content (`mw_ru`, `pwg_ru`) and needing an authoritative, competing-interpretation-
aware gloss for a Russian syntactic term. It is not a source of Sanskrit- or Indology-
specific content — the book contains none — but a general Russian-metalanguage
terminology anchor for translation quality, comparable in role to the other general
Russian-linguistics reference works digested in this same folder.
