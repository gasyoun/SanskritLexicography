## What the work is

*Энциклопедический словарь юного филолога (Языкознание)* ("Encyclopedic dictionary of
the young philologist [Linguistics volume]") is a single-volume popular-science reference
work aimed explicitly at "middle and senior school age" readers (title page: "для среднего
и старшего школьного возраста") — one installment of a well-known Soviet series of "for
young X" encyclopedic dictionaries (parallel volumes existed for other disciplines). Its
own back-cover annotation states the goal plainly: to introduce school-age readers to the
basics of linguistics ("языкознание — науки о языке") — laws of language development,
the languages of the world's peoples, with primary attention to Russian — and to the work
of prominent linguists, framed with an explicit ideological/pedagogical purpose (instilling
pride in one's native language and respect for the languages of other Soviet peoples).

**Structure.** After a signed introductory essay ("О филологии и филологах," by editor-in-
chief Academician G. V. Stepanov) the book has **no separate table of contents** — it
proceeds directly as an alphabetically-ordered sequence of topical entries/articles
(headword-style, e.g. "АГВАНСКОЕ ПИСЬМО," "НАРОДНАЯ ЭТИМОЛОГИЯ," "НАЦИОНАЛЬНЫЙ
ЯЗЫК"), each a short essay (roughly half a page to several pages, occasionally with maps
or illustrations) rather than a terse dictionary-style definition. Entries range across the
full breadth of general and Soviet linguistics: writing systems and their decipherment
(Caucasian Albanian/"Agvan" script, Tangut, Hittite), dialectology, etymology (folk vs.
scientific), sociolinguistics (national language, language policy, language and society),
phonetics/phonology, grammar and parts of speech, language families (Turkic,
Finno-Ugric), language and literature (entries on the language of Tolstoy, Turgenev,
Chekhov), and biographical entries on individual linguists (Shakhmatov, Shcherba,
Ushakov, Fortunatov, Schleicher). The back matter (from roughly line 36,300 on, pp.
~350–352) is a combined alphabetical subject/name index ("Указатель") giving page
references for every entry topic — critically, this index also names **which contributor
wrote which entry** in parentheses next to almost every topic line, functioning as the
de facto table of contents plus author attribution the front of the book lacks.

## Editorial structure and COLLECTIVE AUTHORSHIP — read before any rights judgment

This is not a single-author work and should never be treated as one. Three distinct
layers of contribution are named in the colophon (back matter, ~line 36,455 onward):

1. **Compiler (Составитель):** М. В. Панов — Mikhail Viktorovich Panov, a prominent
   Russian linguist, credited as the overall compiler/organizer of the volume.
2. **Editor-in-chief and editorial board (Редакционная коллегия):** Г. В. Степанов
   (Академик, главный редактор — Academician G. V. Stepanov, editor-in-chief, also author
   of the signed front-matter essay), В. Г. Костомаров (зам. главного редактора —
   deputy editor-in-chief), and board members Ф. Д. Ашнин, С. Г. Бархударов,
   В. А. Белошапкова, Ю. В. Васильев, Е. А. Земская, В. В. Иванов, Л. А. Новиков,
   А. В. Текучёв, Н. И. Толстой, В. С. Хелемендик, plus one further name garbled beyond
   confident OCR recovery in the source scan.
3. **~65 individually named entry-authors (Авторы статей),** each credited by surname in
   the colophon and cross-referenced to their specific entries via the back-index. This
   list includes several figures independently notable in Russian/Soviet linguistics —
   Ю. Д. Апресян (Apresyan), Вяч. Вс. Иванов, А. А. Реформатский, Д. Э. Розенталь,
   И. М. Дьяконов, Л. П. Крысин, В. Г. Гак, among ~55 others — each apparently responsible
   for one or a handful of signed articles (e.g. the index shows "Этимология ... (Ю. В.
   Откупщиков)," "Фонема ... (Л. Л. Касаткин)," "Тюркские языки ... (Ф. Д. Ашнин)").

**Why this matters for rights:** the volume as a whole is a Soviet state-publisher
compilation (editorial/compilation copyright, likely held by or transferred from
Издательство «Педагогика»), but each individual entry may separately carry its named
author's own copyright interest, independent of the compilation right. "Is this encyclopedia
in copyright" and "is this specific entry by [named author] in copyright" are not
necessarily the same question, and this digest does not attempt to resolve either — it only
flags the complexity plainly so a human can make that call with full information, rather
than the collective nature being glossed over.

## Why this work, for this project

Per this repo's own project index, this encyclopedia is used as a **Soviet-era
terminological reference for Russian linguistics vocabulary** — by SaLex (this repo's
research papers) and by the RussianTranslation (RuTrans) subproject when Russian-language
linguistic terms need a period-appropriate, authoritative popular-science gloss (e.g. how
a Soviet linguistics reference of the 1980s defines "фонема," "члены предложения,"
"диалект," "этимология"). It is not used as a source of Sanskrit- or Indology-specific
content — the encyclopedia contains none — but as a general Russian-metalanguage
terminology anchor, comparable in role (though not in rights status) to a general
dictionary consulted for definitional grounding rather than reproduced.
