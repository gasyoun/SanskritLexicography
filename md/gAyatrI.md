[ variously arranged, but generally as a triplet of eight syllables each. ] the Gāyatrī (i.e. ṚV iii, 62, 10). ŚB xiv , ŚāṅGS , Mbh . this is a very sacred verse repeated by every Brāhman at his morning and evening devotions. from being addressed to Savitṛ or the Sun as generator, it is also called Sāvitrī. RTL(MW), S. 19; 342; 361; 403 . the Gāyatrī verse is personified as a goddess, the wife of Brahmā and mother of the four Vedas. Hariv 11666ff . it is often mentioned in connection with the Amṛta, both together constituting as it were the essence and type of sacred hymns in general. AV . the Gāyatrī personified is also considered as the mother of the first three classes in their capacity of twice-born. W(MW) , RTL(MW), S. 200f . some other verse [perhaps ṚV x, 9, 1] is denoted by Gāyatrī.
