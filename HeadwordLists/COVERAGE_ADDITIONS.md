# Coverage additions (PRINT_READINESS item B), ranked by DCS frequency band

Candidate headwords to **add** to the printed [union](union/UNION.md): DCS-corpus lemmas
(attested in real texts) that are in **no** CDSL dictionary. Generated by
[`coverage_additions.py`](coverage_additions.py) → [`union/coverage_additions.tsv`](union/coverage_additions.tsv)
(`dcs_band, kind, slp1, iast`, sorted band 5→1, genuine nominal first). The union's folded
feminines are added back to the baseline so they aren't falsely reported as missing.

## Result

**21,759** DCS-attested lemmas are absent from all 15 CDSL dicts. But the high-frequency end
is dominated by **lemmatisation artifacts**, not real gaps (the same pattern as the
[Catalan §5](Catalan-Pujol/Sanskrit-Catalan-Wordlist-vs-Cologne.md) finding):

| DCS band | total absent | genuine **nominal** | the rest = artifacts |
|--:|--:|--:|---|
| 5 (most frequent) | 14 | **1** | causatives (`dhāray`, `cintay`), bīja (`hrīṃ`), indeclinables |
| 4 | 201 | **6** | prefixed/desiderative roots (`namaskṛ`, `śuśrūṣ`), `kadācid`, `kiṃtu` |
| 3 | 1,097 | **409** | ← the actionable priority set |
| 2 | 6,047 | 4,697 | long tail |
| 1 (rarest) | 14,400 | 12,945 | hapax tail |

`kind` flags the artifacts: `verb-deriv` (`-ay` causatives), `prefixed-verb?`, `verb?`,
`infl?`, `bija/letter`, `indecl?` — DCS lemmatises these as units while CDSL stores them
under the root/components, so they are **not** missing headwords.

## How to use it

- **The real coverage gaps are rare words** — genuine nominal additions concentrate in the
  low bands. High frequency + absent-from-CDSL almost always means a lemmatisation artifact,
  not a gap.
- **Priority add-list = the 409 band-3 nominal** (frequent enough to matter, rare enough to
  be genuine) + the 7 band-4/5 nominal — e.g. `bhasmasūta` (an alchemical mercury
  preparation), `bhṛgutīrtha` (a sacred place), `āntarika`, `dhūmasāra`, `chidramūṣā`.
- **Strongest evidence** is cross-attestation: an addition that is DCS-attested **and** in an
  independent external wordlist ([Catalan-Pujol](Catalan-Pujol/DCS-attested-no-CDSL.md) /
  [Huet](Huet-INRIA-Wordlist-vs-Cologne.md)) but in no CDSL dict — those are the safest adds.
- The `kind` flags are heuristic (shape-based); the editor confirms. Decision (add / don't) is
  human — this just ranks and de-noises the pool.

## Cross-tag with external wordlists (the safest adds)

The strongest add-evidence is **corpus + an independent dictionary** agreeing the word is
real. [`crosstag_additions.py`](crosstag_additions.py) tags the **416** priority additions
(band ≥3, nominal) with whether each is also in the [Catalan-Pujol](Catalan-Pujol/Sanskrit-Catalan-Wordlist-vs-Cologne.md)
and/or [Huet/INRIA](Huet-INRIA-Wordlist-vs-Cologne.md) wordlists →
[`union/coverage_additions_crosstagged.tsv`](union/coverage_additions_crosstagged.tsv)
(ranked by corroboration).

**Only 25 of 416 (6 %) are externally corroborated** — and triaging those 25:

- **~8 genuine real-word candidates** (the actual safe adds): `karkandhū` (a jujube berry),
  `maṇikā` (a small water-jar / measure), `nistaraṅga` (calm, waveless), `cittamātra` (Yogācāra
  "mere thought"), `atigati`, `kāritva`, `hehaya` (the Haihaya), and the comparatives
  `guhyatara`/`guhyatama`.
- **the rest are still artifacts** that slipped the `kind` filter: prefixed/desiderative **verb
  roots** (`vyavasā`, `nidhyā`, `caṅkram`, `vijigīṣ`), **Pāṇinian affixes** (`ghañ`, `ṭhak`,
  `ktvā`), and pronouns (`enad`) — Catalan/Huet headword these too, so corroboration doesn't make
  them nouns.

**Bottom line for B:** even with triple (corpus + 2 external dicts) corroboration, the genuine
real-word additions number **~8**. CDSL's coverage of *attested* vocabulary is essentially
complete; the print list needs almost nothing added. The handful worth considering are rare
nouns (plants, vessels, philosophical terms) — exactly the Catalan §5 profile.
