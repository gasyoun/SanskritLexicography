# Lexicography-Manuals, one at a time — what each gives `pwg_ru` before the bulk run

A per-manual companion to [`LITERATURE_FOR_PWG_RU.md`](LITERATURE_FOR_PWG_RU.md).
That file is organised **by insertion point** (glossary → translator → judge →
corpus-gate → display); this one walks the
[`literature/md/Lexicography-Manuals/`](../literature/md/INDEX.md) shelf **one
manual at a time** and answers a single question for each: *before we start the
real (bulk Max) PWG→Russian run, does this manual change the theory — and where?*

- **Scope:** the 37 files under `Lexicography-Manuals/` only (the syntax-theory
  and Russian-grammar sources live in other INDEX sections and are mined in
  [`LITERATURE_FOR_PWG_RU.md`](LITERATURE_FOR_PWG_RU.md) §1–§7).
- **Source of the content:** the three-pass full-text harvest already done
  2026-06-26. The `.md` shelf files are one-line stubs; the prose lives in the
  PDFs, which the harvest read. This document does **not** re-mine — it re-files
  the harvest verdicts per manual so the pre-run review is auditable book-by-book.
- **Verdict legend:**
  **★ DRIVES THEORY** = a concrete drop-in changes the prompt/glossary/gate ·
  **◐ MARGINAL** = one line, or overlaps a stronger source ·
  **○ OTHER REPO** = real book, but serves DCS / SaLex / csl-atlas, not DE→RU ·
  **⚠ BLOCKED** = can't be mined until re-OCR'd.

## The 19 that change the run (★) — one at a time

### 1. Apresjan · *Systematic Lexicography* (2008) — the keystone
INDEX: **RuTrans**, SaLex, MWS · already has its own brief in
[`APRESJAN.md`](APRESJAN.md).
- **Glossary §1d — the project register tagset (verbatim):** высок./книжн./офиц./
  поэт. · разг./прост. · груб./жарг./бран. · ласк./неодобр./ирон./презр. ·
  устар./арх./ист./нов. · обл./диал. These become the *only* register labels the
  judge recognises.
- **Translator:** force the manner of motion/position Russian demands
  (идти/ползти/лететь; стоять/лежать/висеть) when German leaves it open — a wrong
  default is grammatical-but-false; pick the synonym by register and default to the
  neutral dominant absent a German cue (stops "unmotivatedly elevated" Russian).
- **Judge:** circularity / cryptographic-gloss defect — BAD if the Russian is more
  obscure than the headword or defines a word by itself.
- **Pre-run action:** seed `glossaries/` with §1d; both rules already drafted in
  [`LITERATURE_FOR_PWG_RU.md`](LITERATURE_FOR_PWG_RU.md) §1d/§2/§3.

### 2. Klosa-Kückelhaus · *Internet Lexicography* (2024) — owns the display layer
INDEX: **csl-websanlexicon**, MWS.
- **Cross-refs as data, rendered as one-click links:** every German `s.`/`vgl.`/
  sense-pointer → a resolved hyperlink (distinguish relation vs marker vs target).
- **Search toolkit:** type-ahead, case-insensitive, fuzzy/translit-tolerant,
  wildcard (`-tva`, `√gam`), inflected-form search via lemmatisation.
- **Full-text search over the Russian gloss = a free onomasiological reverse index**
  (Russian concept → Sanskrit word) — a reason to verbalise glosses consistently.
- **Faceted filters** on POS / `<ls>` source / sense-count / root; **QA tool** that
  dumps cross-refs as a graph to find dangling links/orphans.
- **Pre-run action:** none for the *translation* run; this is the spec for the CDSL
  frontend after data exists. Keep glosses link- and search-friendly so it pays off.

### 3. Hartmann & James · *Dictionary of Lexicography* (2002) — equivalence vocabulary
INDEX: SaLex, **MWS**, AP90.
- **Translator:** prefer an accurate discursive paraphrase over a snappy-but-skewed
  single word for culture-bound headwords (the *explanatory equivalent*); preserve
  PWG punctuation semantics — **comma = interchangeable equivalents, semicolon =
  non-interchangeable**; keep PWG's sense order/numbering (reordering is out of scope).
- **Judge:** equivalent-type mismatch (single word forced onto a term needing a
  paraphrase, or needless paraphrase where a clean word exists);
  **polyequivalence / under-differentiation** — every numbered German sub-sense must
  surface a *distinct* Russian equivalent; a dropped register label is a defect
  *separate* from a wrong gloss.
- **Pre-run action:** the four judge defect-classes go into the rubric.

### 4. Riemer · *Routledge Handbook of Semantics* (2015) — the sense-split test battery
INDEX: **SaLex**, MWS. *Highest-value single judge addition.*
- **Judge — sense-distinctness battery** to decide split-vs-merge of two German
  sub-senses: (a) Quinean truth test, (b) zeugma/identity test, (c) definitional
  test; weight by agreement and require a *strong* zeugma clash before splitting
  metaphor/metonymy-linked senses.
- **Autohyponymy is legitimate** (general gloss + narrowed ritual gloss of the same
  word is common in Sanskrit — not circular).
- **Antonym-type-mismatch defect:** комплементарные · градуальные · реверсивы ·
  конверсивы — don't render a converse as a gradable antonym; *nicht lang* ≠
  *короткий*.
- **Translator:** German `un-` forms — follow the form used, don't auto-map to the
  lexical antonym; preserve deliberate vagueness/underspecification.
- **Pre-run action:** the battery + the opposite-type tagset are the marquee judge
  and glossary adds.

### 5. Jackson · *Lexicography: An Introduction* (2002) — definition typology + homonyms
INDEX: **SaLex**, MWS, AP90.
- **Translator:** function-word "use" definitions (German *dient zum Ausdruck…*) →
  Russian function-paraphrase (*служит для выражения…*), never coerced into a noun.
- **Judge:** respect source homonym numbering (splitting is etymology-driven — don't
  merge two superscript-numbered headwords on gloss-similarity); *gewöhnlich/meist*
  is a hedge on a core sense, **not** a new sub-sense.
- **Glossary:** German register-cue → Apresjan-tag crosswalk
  (*abwertend/scherzh./iron.* → §1d label); sense-relation marker rows
  (*auch genannt* → *также называется*).

### 6. Gonda & Vogel · *History of Indian Literature, Vol 5* (kośa tradition)
INDEX: SaLex, **PWG**, MWS.
- **Corpus-gate (Vogel's kośa caveats):** a sense sourced to a synonymic *kośa* has
  **weaker** grounding than a cited passage (confidence signal); kośa sense-order is
  topic-grouping, **not** frequency (so sense #1 ≠ "most common"); dual/plural in a
  homonymic kośa entry is a **sense-count artifact** (dual = 2 senses), not
  morphology — don't render literally; exclude metrical filler particles from the
  translatable span; a long German synonym chain may be an unresolved *dvandva* →
  flag for sense-splitting.

### 7. Baalbaki · *The Arabic Lexicographical Tradition* (2014)
INDEX: **SaLex**.
- **Translator — synonym-string cardinality:** render a German synonym-string
  (*Glanz, Schimmer, Pracht*) as a Russian synonym-string of **equal cardinality**;
  never collapse to one word.
- **Corpus-gate — quote-count confidence:** a sense backed by a single `<ls>` siglum
  is less grounded than a multiply-attested one; count distinct sigla.

### 8. Ferri · *The Latin of Roman Lexicography*
INDEX: **SaLex**.
- **Translator — never abridge an entry:** a shorter Russian entry is a defect.
  (Paul-the-Deacon's compression of Festus destroyed the very sense/form distinctions
  the source drew.) Preserve every sub-sense and source context.
- **Corpus-gate — transmission-depth signal:** a visibly second-hand gloss (copied
  from an earlier kośa/lexicon) gets lower confidence; don't present it as primary
  attestation.

### 9. Dickey · *Ancient Greek Scholarship*
INDEX: SaLex, AP90, **MWS**.
- **Translator/judge — don't harmonize a multi-source entry** into one smooth Russian
  register: PWG entries are stratified accretions; preserve the seams (smoothing is
  the error).
- **Judge — the citation is an independent witness:** never "correct" a `<ls>` quote
  to match a suspect gloss; if gloss and quote disagree, the quote usually wins.
- **Glossary — controlled Russian metalanguage** for apparatus terms (gloss vs
  definition vs citation vs note); fix it once and enforce it.

### 10. Inglese & Geupel · *The encoding of ad hoc categories in Sanskrit* (2018)
INDEX: **csl-atlas**, MWS, DCS.
- **Translator/glossary — `-ādi`/`-prabhṛti` compounds** = open-ended category = the
  *hypernym* of members → «X и тому подобное / и прочее», never a closed dvandva list.

### 11. Lowe · *Participles in Rigvedic Sanskrit*
INDEX: GRA, **WhitneyRoots**, csl-atlas. *(Pass-1 judged "marginal"; pass-3 mined a
real gate rule.)*
- **Corpus-gate (keying) — lexicalized participle ≠ live participle:** a
  `-ant/-vant/-ta` surface form (e.g. *sunvánt-* "Soma-presser") can attest the verb's
  sense **or** a lexicalized nominal sense — don't collapse them onto one headword
  key; flag `-ta/-na/-ant` as POS-ambiguous when keying.

### 12. Allan · *Routledge Handbook of Linguistics* (2016)
INDEX: **SaLex**.
- **Display/learner — sense-ordering policy:** PWG's order is genealogical (19th-c.
  historical dict); a modern pwg_ru *may* re-sort by frequency/generality for the
  reader, **but** keep it an explicit, flagged transform, never silent
  (consistent with the project's "Renou = badge, never re-sort" rule for the data
  layer). Mediostructure: attach paradigm + KWIC concordance links around the gloss.

### 13. Szudarski · *Corpus Linguistics for Vocabulary* (2017)
INDEX: DCS, **RuTrans**.
- **Display/learner — coverage-tier profiling:** compute SLP1-lemma coverage over the
  aligned corpus to bucket headwords into core / mid-frequency / specialist tiers; the
  **mid-frequency band is the learner layer's primary target**.

### 14. Desagulier · *Corpus Linguistics and Statistics with R*
INDEX: **csl-observatory**, DCS.
- **Corpus-gate — association measures, not raw counts:** rank a candidate Russian
  sense by association strength of its collocates (PMI / log-likelihood G² / Fisher
  exact); do **not** read high raw frequency as association.

### 15. Egbert / Larsson / Biber · *Doing Linguistics with a Corpus*
INDEX: **DCS**, csl-atlas.
- **Corpus-gate — dispersion test:** require a Russian sense to attest across
  *multiple* corpus texts (good dispersion), not just clear a token threshold; reject
  senses carried by one outlier; compute normalized per-stratum rates, not pooled
  totals. Rater-agreement check on human-verified gate output.

### 16. Biagetti & Hellwig · *Evaluating Syntactic Annotation* (2021)
INDEX: **DCS**, csl-atlas.
- **Human-verify — Jaccard span-IoU metric:** measure agreement on *which Sanskrit
  token-span* a Russian sense attaches to (intersection-over-union), since unit
  boundaries vary and label-kappa doesn't apply; report `raw → cleaned →
  cleaned-sameSeg`; budget more review on prose strata (lower reliability than metrical).

### 17. Ryan · *Handbook of Corpus Linguistics* (2019)
INDEX: **DCS**, csl-observatory.
- **Corpus-gate — decision-list WSD with positional weighting:** weight disambiguating
  collocates by offset/distance (Smadja-style), not flat PMI, when picking the cue for
  which sense a usage supports.

### 18. Sinclair · *How to Use Corpora in Language Teaching*
INDEX: **DCS**, RuTrans. *(Teaching framing is ○; the collocate principle is ★.)*
- **Corpus-gate — sense-by-distinct-collocate-set:** two proposed Russian senses
  drawing on the *same* Sanskrit collocate set are likely one sense (merge); genuinely
  distinct senses show distinct collocates — a concrete split/merge cross-check.

### 19. Keller · *Programming for Corpus Linguistics with Python* (2024)
INDEX: DCS, csl-observatory, **RuTrans**.
- **Corpus-gate (engineering) — range via `nunique()`** as a fast pre-filter (how many
  texts/strata a sense appears in) before the heavier dispersion test (#15).

## Bottom line for the pre-run gate

those drop-ins is already distilled into
[`LITERATURE_FOR_PWG_RU.md`](LITERATURE_FOR_PWG_RU.md) and queued as the

decision to start the run isn't taken with an unexamined book on the shelf.
