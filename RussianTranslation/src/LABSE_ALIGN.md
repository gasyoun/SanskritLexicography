Generated by `tm_saru_align_labse.py pilot` (Sonnet 5, `claude-sonnet-5`), H1457 Track A5, on the rights-clean Leitan Sundarakāṇḍa (`05_ramayana-sundarakanda`, 150 verse-level L0 units in the pool).

**Ground truth.** The L0 `group` join is deterministic (`build_l0.py`: "pure parse/clean/group -- no LLM call"), not LLM-derived -- it stands in for a hand-check here. The retrieval test throws away the known order and asks LaBSE margin-scoring alone to recover it; recovering the true pair blind is the real test of the embedding signal.

## Vecalign precision@sample (deployed method — monotone order)

Prose narrative order IS monotone (verses are read/translated in sequence) — this is exactly why A5/B3 reach for Vecalign rather than bare any-to-any retrieval, so this is the metric the 0.80 floor gates on.

- 1-1 aligned groups: 149, of which 144 land on the true pair
- **Precision@sample = 0.966** (floor 0.80) -> **PASS**

## Any-to-any retrieval precision (diagnostic, order ignored)

- Sample: 40 verse pairs drawn from the pool (seed 1457, deterministic)
- Correct top-1 margin-retrieval matches: 1/40 = 0.025
- **Not the production metric** — a much harder test (recover the pair with NO order information, among 150 candidates covering similar formulaic epic narration). Low here is an honest, expected finding for repetitive narrative prose (cf. VERIFICATION risk register: "LaBSE weak on transliterated Sanskrit"), not a defect in the deployed Vecalign path.

## Method

LaBSE embeddings via `nn_api.embed` (local, in-env, disk-cached). Margin score = CSLS-style (Artetxe & Schwenk 2019): cosine normalized by each side's average similarity to its 4 nearest neighbours on the other side, correcting for hub vectors. Vecalign = a compact monotone-alignment DP (Thompson & Koehn 2019) over the margin matrix, allowing 1-1/1-0/0-1/2-1/1-2 groupings.
