# Cross-dictionary sense-order metrics (executed 2026-06-24)

Companion to [sense_order_metrics.md](sense_order_metrics.md) (PWG-only). Generated by
[analyze_cross_dict.py](analyze_cross_dict.py); raw [cross_dict_metrics.json](cross_dict_metrics.json).
Each dict is segmented on its OWN microstructure (MW: <L> records sharing k1+k2+hom;
AP90: {@N@} markers; Koch: one gloss/record) and dated via the matching Renou map
(ls_source_map.json / _mw.json); AP90 has no map so it is measured by Vedic-siglum density.

## The discriminator: Vedic-citation density

Fraction of *cited* senses that cite at least one Vedic (Renou state I) source.

| Dict | Vedic density | basis (cited senses) | reading |
|---|---|---|---|
| **PWG** | **23.4%** | 113,012 | strong historical apparatus |
| **MW**  | **24.8%** | 99,716 | strong historical apparatus |
| **AP90**| **2.3%** | 19,163 | **~10x lower — no historical apparatus** |
| **Koch**| **0.0%** | 29,177 records | **0 citations — logical-semantic by construction** |

PWG and MW cluster tightly (~1/4 of cited senses reach back to the Veda); AP90 drops an
order of magnitude (2.3%); Kochergina has no dated citations at all (5/29,177 records).

## #1 / #3 (where datable)

| Metric | PWG | MW |
|---|---|---|
| Multi-sense dated entries | 11,882 | 13,822 |
| **Printed sense-1 = oldest** | 73.5% | **69.4%** |
| **Kendall tau (printed vs date)** | 0.375 | **0.367** |
| Within-sense strict-chrono | 25.4% | 55.0% |
| Adjacent cite pairs non-decreasing | 76.8% | 67.6% |

MW tracks PWG almost exactly on the cross-sense axis (sense-1-oldest 69.4% vs 73.5%;
tau 0.367 vs 0.375) — **the same genetic, moderately-date-correlated regime.** MW's higher
*within-sense* strict-chronology (55.0%) is mostly granularity: an MW "sense" is one short
record with few citations, so monotonicity is easier to hit than in PWG's long pooled senses
— not evidence of stricter editorial dating.

- AP90 #1/#3 are **not computable** (no dated-citation apparatus to sort on) — which is
  itself the finding: Apte (16,005 multi-sense entries) orders by salience, not date.
- Koch: 37.9% of glosses carry numbered senses but none are dated.

## Verdict

Vedic density alone separates the two camps cleanly: **PWG≈MW (23-25%) >> AP90 (2.3%) >> Koch (0%)**.
Combined with sense-1-oldest (PWG 73.5% ≈ MW 69.4%) this confirms quantitatively: the 19th-c.
European dicts are **etymological-genetic with a real Vedic-anchored historical apparatus**;
Apte and Kochergina are **logical-semantic / pedagogical** with little-to-no historical layer.
