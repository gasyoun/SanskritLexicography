## 4. Compatibility constraints

1. **H255 / no-PWG is FROZEN.** No correction drains, requeues, re-offers or re-plans a no_pwg
   key. C-49's denylist backfill is *additive* (more keys skipped, never fewer). C-03's stamp fix
   must not alter the no_pwg lane's behaviour — it is the one lane where the gate works today, and
   changing it would break the only historical series this gate has.
2. **The 30 s default gate must not be rewritten as part of a correction.**
   `LATENCY_CEIL_MS = 30_000` and `CONN_ERR_CEIL` stay. C-07 and C-08 change *storage and
   recomputation of a verdict*, never the threshold. Any argument that schema-carrying calls
   deserve a different ceiling — asserted in prose on JSONL line 14 and repeated at
   `KILL_CALIBRATION_REAUDIT_2026-07-09.md:30` and `.ai_state.md:522`, and existing as **no code
   constant anywhere** — is an open policy question **a human decides**, not a correction.
3. **`KILL_CEIL_MS = 180000` is an MG ruling.** Recorded in-code at `gen_opt_harness2.py:183-184`.
   C-15 adds telemetry so a size-doomed kill is separable from a route-slow one; it does not move
   the ceiling. Rebalancing it against the size distribution (the nominal core's raw median is
   12 556 B, far above the 1 555.6 B saturation point) is a human decision with real cost
   consequences. C-48 fixes the *doc* to match the code, never the reverse.
4. **Language-parity hashes need separate human reaffirmation.** The 3 stale ledger entries
   (C-27) are two **SHARED** verdicts. `--update-hash` re-asserts that a fix landed in both
   languages. An agent running it to make CI green would fabricate exactly the assurance the
   ledger exists to provide. Reaffirmation first, hash second.
5. **The linked-worktree store resolver exists for the H255/H805 loss fix and must not be naively
   "fixed".** `canonical_store()` resolving a linked worktree's store to the MAIN checkout is
   **by design** (`store_path.py:2-31` records the incident: "Every promotion in that worktree
   vanished silently"). Its env-override-first precedence is deliberate and selftest-locked. C-20
   and C-21 add *disclosure* ("git failed to answer" ≠ "this is the main checkout") and *refusal*;
   they do not touch precedence. Corollary: **an isolated worktree is not isolated for the store**
   — every generation/preflight/audit step must keep pinning `PWG_RU_STORE` to a scratch copy, and
   C-21 exists precisely because that pin is honoured with no existence check.
6. **Immutable evidence stays immutable.** The four H963 pilot artifacts must stay byte-identical
   to their recorded hashes (`manifest.canary.json` `7fbb7b74…`, `manifest.real.json` `5a438e34…`,
   and the two harnesses). Append-only logs are corrected by appending. `campaign_windows.jsonl`
   rows are amended by a new row + provenance note, never rewritten in place — C-25's retraction
   is an append.
7. **No reroll.** Re-running the H963 pilot to obtain serial numbers is prohibited. Any serial
   measurement is a **fresh, owner-authorised attempt**, not a reroll of this one.
8. **Behaviour-identical requirements, so historical comparisons stay valid.**

## 6. Recommended implementation order

Nine steps. The ordering is not by severity — it is by **dependency**: several corrections are
unsafe or unmeasurable until an earlier one lands.

### Step 1 — Provenance and lane discriminators (C-29, C-30, C-31, C-32, + C-39's `validation_method`)

**First, because it is the precondition for measuring anything else.** C-16 and C-17's blast radius
is *unmeasurable today* — `meta.generator` reads `gen_opt_harness2.batched-masked` on both lanes,
so the store cannot be partitioned. C-05's promoter refusal needs a synthetic class to refuse on.
C-11's honest answer ("refuse to label a Workflow run with a profile it cannot back") needs an
`execution_route`. Schema-additive only: **adding the fields changes no behaviour**, which is
exactly why it is safe to go first. Land the whole family in one bump. Record explicitly that
historical rows stay unclassifiable.

### Step 2 — The store-corruption backfill and its gate (C-02 → C-01 → C-42 → C-04)

**Strictly ordered inside the step.** C-02 first: fix the three stitch constructors, *then*
backfill the 468 `h == null` rows — the 65 `topup` rows re-propagate a None `h` from upstream, so
backfilling before the harness fix re-introduces them. Then C-01: shared field-set constant,
extended restore, `{Tn}`-residue refusal, then the offline re-derivation of the 668 leaked rows via
`pwg_mask.mask(raw)` keyed by `input_raw_sha256`. Then C-42: the 2 out-of-range rows are **not**
re-derivable and need re-translation — park them, do not silently leave them. **Only then C-04**:
wiring the validator before the backfill would red the chain on the 20 subcards already in the
store. This step ends with the store clean and the contract mechanically enforced.

### Step 3 — Close the promotion loss and fabrication paths (C-05, C-06, C-36, C-43; then C-20, C-37, C-38)

C-06 and C-36 share a fix: replacing the coordinator's global line-count delta with a per-lease
subcard assertion closes the `store_before=0` blind spot **and** makes crash-recovery a correct
no-op instead of a wedge — do them as one change, not two. C-05 rides on Step 1's synthetic class;
C-43 is the cosmetic twin and lands with it. Then the path-resolution family (C-20, C-37, C-38) —
all "resolve to one canonical location, disclose, refuse", none touching the precedence or the
transaction logic they sit next to. After this step the canonical store has no known silent-loss or
silent-fabrication path.

### Step 4 — The launch gate (C-07, C-08; then C-28)

C-07 and C-08 are the same file and the same contract ("a verdict is derived, not asserted") —
land together, with the new `probe_log_selftest.py`. C-28 is the same shape one layer up (a guard
that silently does not run). **Nothing here touches the 30 s ceiling.** Note what this step does
*not* settle: the schema-carrying-ceiling carve-out asserted in prose on line 14 has no code
constant and stays a human decision.

### Step 5 — Account binding and admission (C-09, C-10, C-11, C-12, C-13, C-53)

**Blocked on a human design decision, and deliberately late.** C-09/C-10/C-11 interact: a
name-based allow-list would enforce a label C-09 shows is unverified, so "allow-list by name vs pin
by verified identity vs refuse-unless-single-account" must be settled **once, by a human**, for all
three. C-12/C-13 are the admission half and follow the same ruling (route launches through the
claim path, or give the Workflow route an equivalent). C-53 is gated on establishing CLI auth
precedence, which cannot be done without either documentation or a live experiment — a human
decides whether that experiment is worth an attempt while the latency gate stands at NO-GO. C-09
also carries a hard unknown: whether `auth status --json` even emits a comparable identity field.

### Step 6 — Guard parity and kill observability (C-16, C-17, C-15, C-33, C-46, C-47)

Rides on Step 1 (attribution) and Step 4 (a gate that cannot be silently overridden). C-17's
one-specification fix should land before or with C-16's port, so the ported guard is not ported
wrong. All of it is observability: **`SANLOSS_HARD_REJECT` and `TNMASK_HARD_REJECT` stay `false`**,
