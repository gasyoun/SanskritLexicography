# Data Statement — `pwg_ru` AI-Translated Russian Dictionary Store

_Created: 07-07-2026 · Last updated: 07-07-2026_

This document is a data statement in the sense of Bender & Friedman (2018) for the
**pwg_ru output dataset**: the local, content-addressed store of Russian translations of
Petersburg-tradition Sanskrit dictionary entries, produced by the frequency-ordered
LLM translation pipeline in this repository. It complements — and does not repeat — the
per-sidecar [`TRANSLATION_MEMORY_DATASHEET.md`](https://github.com/gasyoun/SanskritLexicography/blob/master/RussianTranslation/TRANSLATION_MEMORY_DATASHEET.md)
(Gebru et al. 2021 "Datasheets for Datasets" style, adapted): that template governs the
translation-memory *sidecars* derived from this store; this statement describes the
*store itself*. Store facts below are as of the
[H178 re-audit, 06-07-2026](https://github.com/gasyoun/SanskritLexicography/blob/master/RussianTranslation/pwg_ru/H178_REAUDIT_2026-07-06.md):
**11,261 rows across 50 roots-or-window-keys**, all `review_status=ai_translated`, held for
the G5 human-review gate.

## A. Curation rationale

There is no complete Russian edition of the Petersburg tradition — the foundational
Sanskrit–German lexicographic corpus (Böhtlingk–Roth and successors) remains inaccessible
to Russian-speaking students and scholars except through German or through the much smaller
Kochergina dictionary. This dataset exists to produce one: a printed/electronic **PWG→Russian
edition**, built card-by-card from the digitized source.

The curation strategy is **harvest-first, frequency-ordered**: verb roots (and nominal
window keys) are drained in DCS corpus-frequency order via a reproducible worklist
([`verb_worklist.py`](https://github.com/gasyoun/SanskritLexicography/blob/master/RussianTranslation/src/pilot/verb_worklist.py):
749 attested verb roots), so the most-attested vocabulary is translated first. Every card
is translated by an LLM under a token-optimized QA regime ("Python at max, LLM at minimum" —
see [`RUN_FREQ_MAX.md`](https://github.com/gasyoun/SanskritLexicography/blob/master/RussianTranslation/src/pilot/RUN_FREQ_MAX.md)),
then held in the store as machine output awaiting human review. Machine translation is the
*drafting* mechanism, not the editorial verdict: nothing in this store is print-ready until
the G5 gate passes.

## B. Language variety

**Source language.** 19th-century scholarly German in the Böhtlingk–Roth lexicographic
register — telegraphic sense definitions, abbreviated citations, philological apparatus —
spanning the 1855–1928 layers of the Petersburg tradition. Since commit `1dad0dd`
(17-06-2026) the translated source is the **5-layer all-in-one** built by
[`_pilot_gen_merged.py`](https://github.com/gasyoun/SanskritLexicography/blob/master/RussianTranslation/src/_pilot_gen_merged.py):
PWG main + Nachträge, PW, Schmidt's Nachträge (SCH), PWK Verbesserungen/Nachträge (PWKVN),
and NWS (owner-mapped). The H178 re-audit confirmed **all 50 promoted roots post-date the
merge** (0 pure-PWG pre-merge roots). Embedded throughout is **Sanskrit in SLP1 encoding**
(headwords, citation forms, compound members), plus citation abbreviations to Sanskrit
primary texts.

**Target language.** Modern scholarly Russian in the register of Russian indological
lexicography (Kochergina-adjacent terminology, curated Sanskrit-to-Russian terminology
evidence per the TM datasheet's rules — raw MW English is never RU evidence).

## C. Generator / annotator provenance

Bender & Friedman's "speaker/annotator demographic" is adapted here, since the "speakers"
are 19th-century lexicographers and the translation labor is machine:

- **Generation model:** Sonnet 5 (`claude-sonnet-5`), uniformly — the per-root harness
  generated by [`gen_opt_harness2.py`](https://github.com/gasyoun/SanskritLexicography/blob/master/RussianTranslation/src/pilot/gen_opt_harness2.py)
  hardcodes `model:'sonnet'` on every agent call, regardless of the orchestrating session's
  model. Verified: all 11,261 rows carry exact `provenance.model_version=claude-sonnet-5`
  and the H170 `provenance.pipeline` stamp (1.0.0/1.0.0/1.0.0), 0 stale.
- **Deterministic QA (0 tokens, 100 % of cards):** four Python gates via
  [`audit_window.py`](https://github.com/gasyoun/SanskritLexicography/blob/master/RussianTranslation/src/pilot/audit_window.py)
  — NWS owner-map, markup fidelity, sense coverage, sense-duplicate — the canonical bulk
  acceptance mechanism.
- **LLM judge lane (the only QA spend):** gate-flagged cards plus a deterministic ~10 %
  sample; Sonnet judges, Opus adjudicates only its rejects, per
  [`JUDGE_POLICY.md`](https://github.com/gasyoun/SanskritLexicography/blob/master/RussianTranslation/research/JUDGE_POLICY.md).
- **Human review:** a single human reviewer (the maintainer) at the **G5 gate — pending**.
  No row in the store has yet passed human review; `review_status=ai_translated` throughout.
  G6 (human gold), G7 (double review), and G10 (edition cut) are further downstream.

## D. Text characteristics

Rows are **dictionary sub-card sense units**, not running prose. Each row pairs a rendered
source sense with its Russian translation and provenance. Characteristic structure:

- `{#...#}` — SLP1-encoded Sanskrit (headwords, forms, compounds);
- `{%...%}` — italicized glosses/emphases from the print;
- `<ls>...</ls>` — literature-source citations (abbreviated Sanskrit text references),
  preserved verbatim under the markup-fidelity gate;
- card-ID **layer suffixes** routing the 5 source layers: `_zz_pw`, `_zz_sch`, `_zz_pwkvn`,
  `_zz_nws00` (base IDs = PWG main+Nachträge);
- per-row provenance: root, card/fragment address, raw input SHA-256, `generated_at`,
  model version, pipeline stamp, gate/review status, `partial:true` where applicable
  (70 partial-card rows as of the re-audit).

Sense length ranges from one-line glosses to dense multi-hundred-`<ls>` verb-root articles
(oversized cards are presplit into a selfheal lane by the harness).

## E. Intended uses

1. **The PWG→Russian edition** (printed and electronic) — the primary product, after G5+
   human gates pass.
2. **Translation-memory reuse** — content-addressed exact/fragment/publication TM sidecars
   (2,302 / 217 / 2,392 cards as of the re-audit) under the gating rules of the
   [TM datasheet](https://github.com/gasyoun/SanskritLexicography/blob/master/RussianTranslation/TRANSLATION_MEMORY_DATASHEET.md):
   exact reuse only through content hashes and trust gates; fuzzy evidence is advisory only.
3. **LLM-lexicography research** — the store's uniform provenance stamps, deterministic
   gate ledgers, and judge verdicts make it a case study in auditable machine translation
   of legacy scholarly dictionaries (paper track: H178 Part B).

**Non-intended uses** mirror the TM datasheet: citing machine-only rows as print-ready,
treating `ai_translated` rows as human-approved, or redistributing before rights clearance.

## F. Limitations

- **Superseded philology.** The source is 19th-century scholarship; Vedic senses in
  particular reflect pre-modern Vedic philology and are translated *as the source states
  them*, not corrected to current scholarly consensus. The edition inherits this by design.
- **No row is human-approved yet.** All 11,261 rows are `review_status=ai_translated`;
  the G5 human-review gate (single reviewer) is pending. The H178 re-audit's forensic
  current-gate sweep flagged 16 promoted low-confidence keys + 214 high-confidence
  semantic-risk keys as the deliberate oversample for the Part B-1 human-evaluation sample.
- **1 documented partial-card residual class:** `dah~~h0_zz_pw` fragment
  `dah~~h0_zz_pw__s10p0` hard-failed the StructuredOutput retry cap on both fresh attempts
  and is promoted as a 🟡 residual (`partial:true`), per the 2-attempt cap and the `vid`
  precedent.
- **9 legacy SHA-less `vid` rows** (the H188 stripped-meta autosplit finding, fixed forward
  in [PR #197](https://github.com/gasyoun/SanskritLexicography/pull/197)) carry no
  `generated_at` / input SHAs; content spot-checked complete but they are excluded from
  content-addressed TM reuse.
- **Source-render drift.** Promoted rows were gated against the source render current at
  generation time; the render has since evolved (e.g. `{{Lbody}}` resolution,
  [PR #183](https://github.com/gasyoun/SanskritLexicography/pull/183)), so store-vs-current
  sense-count drift is a known property, not a defect — G5 re-renders from the store.
- **Local-only distribution.** The store is not published — rights clearance
  (in-copyright evidence sources, release-safety review) must precede any release.
- **No automatic-metric or human quality evaluation yet.** COMETKiwi reference-free QE and
  the structured human evaluation are pending (H178 Part B-1); until then quality claims
  rest on the deterministic gates + judge lane only.
