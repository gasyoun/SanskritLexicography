## Current verdict

H442's stop condition is **not met**.

- h317_w1b re-run result: `3/12` clean, `budget_kill_switch_tripped=true`.
- Per-card heal cap did **not** bind: `0` per-card-budget fires and `0`
  partial cards.
- Run was infra-confounded: `3` connection errors and `58/61` kill-timeouts.
- PR #301's per-card heal budget is useful as a guardrail, but it is **not
  sufficient** for the medium50 lane.

The new blocker is **kill-timeout x bisection waste under slow calls**. When a
group times out, `healGroup` currently bisects it as if the group were too large
or malformed. Under slow calls this is pure spend: smaller fragments hit the
same timeout floor, and the window consumes `MAX_AGENTS` without translating the
remaining cards.

## P0 - no bisection after kill-timeout

**Goal:** a kill-timeout should fail fast to requeue, not recursively spawn
smaller heal calls.

**Change:**

- In `healGroup`, distinguish kill-timeout from ordinary malformed/missing or
  fidelity failures.
- After the first kill-timeout for a heal group, mark that group's unresolved
  fragments as missing/requeue candidates and stop spending heal calls on that
  group.
- Do not recurse into `label + '/A'` / `label + '/B'` after a kill-timeout.
- Keep recursive bisection for non-timeout failures where smaller groups can
  plausibly help: malformed response, missing fragment key, mismatched fragment
  key, or fragment-fidelity rejection.

**Done when:**

- A synthetic harness/selftest proves a kill-timeout path returns missing
  fragments without bisection.
- Existing per-card heal budget behavior remains enabled and covered.
- `LANG_PARITY.md` records the behavior as SHARED, because `healGroup` is
  language-agnostic.
