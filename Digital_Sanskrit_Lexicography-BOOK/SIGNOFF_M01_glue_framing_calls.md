## The calls

| # | Where | The call as drafted | Why, and the alternative |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Introduction §1 | The book opens on an invented vignette: *agni* answered by the MW entry and by the Amarakośa fire-verse ("Ask two dictionaries what fire is"). | A scene, not data — the most authorial move in the draft. The example reuses Ch. 4's own fire-verse illustration for consistency. Alternative: open on the thesis statement directly (soberer, closer to the chapters' register). |
| 2 | Introduction §3 | The A61-seeded survival argument keeps two coarse project dates (1994 founding, 2014 move to public versioning) but **no names, no quoted testimony, no funding history**; the institutional losses are described generically ("the closure of the university department that founded it", "the loss of one of its architects"). | The ruling takes A61's *argument* and leaves the chronicle. The two dates are project-defining public facts; everything nameable was left out so no A61 permission gate is touched. Alternative: strip the dates too, or (opposite direction) permit named acknowledgment — a human should decide where the chronicle line sits. |
| 3 | Introduction §3 | The book states its own sobering converse: Sanskrit lexicography still works to mid-19th-century standards, and the book claims the substrate, **not** the corpus-revolution transition. | Drafted as a credibility move (under-claiming). A referee could still quote it against the book's ambition. Alternative: soften to a future-work note in the Conclusion only. |
| 4 | Introduction §4 | The graph-is-not-the-news concession is made at full strength, by name, against the same-series *Internet Lexicography* (LSM 164): "Anyone claiming 'the dictionary as a graph' as a discovery in 2026 would be reinventing the encoding layer." | Pre-empts the strongest referee attack (crosswalk §3) by conceding it before claiming the epistemics delta. Alternative: a quieter positioning paragraph without the sharp sentence. |
| 5 | Introduction §4 | Piotrowski's "is there a theory?" is answered with a deliberately modest claim: not a theory of lexicography, but "a theory of the evidential status of lexicographic statements." | The crosswalk mandates engaging the theory-question; this is the narrowest honest answer. Alternative: claim the fuller "concrete answer" framing the crosswalk sketches. |
| 6 | Introduction §7 + bridges II/IV | Ch. 3 and Ch. 11 are described **only at the plan of record's altitude** (their scope, not their findings), and both bridges carry a boxed ⚠️ obligation to re-describe them against the chapters as merged (H1240). | The chapters do not exist yet; describing them from the plan alone was barred by the handoff. The ⚠️ boxes are removable only by that revision pass. |
| 7 | Introduction §8 | A compact conventions note (sigla, IAST/SLP1, "the corpus", chapter-internal numbering) sits as the Introduction's last section. | Alternative: move to separate front matter ("Note on conventions") at production; the content is the same either way. |
| 8 | Part III bridge | The comparative "Sanskrit among the lexicographical civilizations" upgrade is placed at **Part III** (per the H1241 work order), structured §1 three axes (order / witness / copying, Baalbaki load-bearing) + §2 chapter walk — although crosswalk §4.1 called Part IV its most natural home. The bridge runs ~2× the length of the others. | The mid-book position lets the three axes point backward (order → Part II) and forward (witness/copying → Part IV). Alternative: move the civilizational §1 to open Part IV and shrink Part III's bridge to the §2 chapter walk. |
| 9 | Part IV bridge + Conclusion | Ch. 12's verdict formula — "heir of the scholarship, author of the prose" — recurs in both, making it a book-level motif rather than a chapter-local phrase. | Deliberate repetition-as-motif. Alternative: keep it unique to Ch. 12 and paraphrase elsewhere. |
| 10 | Conclusion §3 | The book names its own weakest evidence layers in print: the unminted DOIs ("a book about evidence should name its own weakest evidence-layer, and this is it") and, in §5, the n=1 reviewer / unmeasured κ gap. | Maximal-honesty posture, consistent with the thesis; but it commits the printed book to a self-criticism the FAIR sprint (deferred to ~09-08-2026) may resolve before submission — if DOIs are minted first, §3 needs a rewrite, not a deletion. |
| 11 | Conclusion §5 | The closing horizon is the community-validated dictionary of record — the author's long-standing programme, stated without chronicle — and the final cadence is "The evidence graph is the same lesson, taught to machines — and through them, once more, to readers." | The most voice-exposed sentence in the draft. Alternative endings: close on the two-civilizations verdict (§1's last paragraph), or on the honest-limits paragraph. |
| 12 | All seven files | Lengths as drafted: Introduction ≈ 14 pp. (budget 15–20), Conclusion ≈ 11 pp. (budget 10–15), bridges ≈ 2–3 pp. each with Part III ≈ 5 pp. | Under-budget by design — density over padding. If the author wants the Introduction at full budget, the natural expansions are §2 (the two civilizations' historiography) and §5 (worked scoping examples). |

## What is NOT open here

The chapters' findings, numbers and reading rules are quoted from the twelve committed
chapters and are not this draft's to change; the A61 rights boundary (no quotations, no
chronicle, book-cites-nothing / A61-cites-book) is a standing MG ruling, not a call; and
the 14-chapter / 5-part architecture is locked
([BOOK_PLAN.md §3](https://github.com/gasyoun/SanskritLexicography/blob/master/Digital_Sanskrit_Lexicography-BOOK/BOOK_PLAN.md)).
