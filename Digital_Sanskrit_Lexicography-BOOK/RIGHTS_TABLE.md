## The headline finding (why this table is *simpler* than it looks)

**None of the 15 source articles is published yet** — every one is a draft at readiness
2/5–5/5 (pre-submission or "ready to send"; see
[Uprava/ARTICLES.md](https://github.com/gasyoun/Uprava/blob/main/ARTICLES.md)). Therefore:

1. **Copyright is 100% author-held today.** No journal owns any of this text, so at the
   present moment the book can quote all 15 freely — there is no permission to obtain *right
   now*.
2. **The permission question crystallizes per-article, at the moment each article is
   accepted** by a journal — not before. Until an article is accepted, its row here is
   "author-held, no action."
3. **The book runs in parallel with the articles** (MG's framing,
   [BOOK_PLAN.md §8](https://github.com/gasyoun/SanskritLexicography/blob/master/Digital_Sanskrit_Lexicography-BOOK/BOOK_PLAN.md)),
   so some articles will publish before the book and some after. Only the *before* cases
   invoke a journal CTA; the *after* cases never leave author copyright at all.
4. **Reusing one's own text in one's own later monograph is the norm** and, per Brill's own
   guidance, needs **no written permission** — the obligation is *disclosure + acknowledgment*,
   not clearance. The residual risk is confined to a small number of licence-to-publish
   (not CC-BY) venues.

So the real deliverable is not "collect 15 permissions" — it is a **per-venue self-reuse
policy map** plus a **trigger rule**: when article X is accepted at venue Y, record Y's
self-reuse clause in the row before the chapter ships.

## Venue self-reuse policy map

The 15 articles target six venue families. Their author-self-reuse posture:

| Venue family | Publisher | Licence model | Author reuse in own monograph | Risk |

Series Maior** parent journal / **Lexicographica** yearbook | De Gruyter (De Gruyter Brill imprint) | Author grants publishing right; self-reuse in later book permitted with acknowledgment | Permitted; **in-house** if the book lands at De Gruyter LSM (the primary target) | **Very low** — same house as the book |
| **Beiträge zur Geschichte der Sprachwissenschaft** (A36) | Nodus Publikationen | Small-press CTA; author self-reuse customarily permitted | Permitted with acknowledgment (confirm exact CTA wording at acceptance) | **Low** — confirm CTA text |
| **Lexikos**, **eLex** | AOSIS / trans-European e-lex | Open Access, CC-BY | Author retains copyright; reuse fully permitted with attribution | **None** |
| **LREC-COLING** (A12) | ELRA / ACL (ACL Anthology) | Open Access, CC-BY | Author retains copyright; reuse fully permitted with attribution | **None** |
| **JOHD**, **Journal of Cultural Analytics** | Ubiquity Press / OA | Open Access, CC-BY | Author retains copyright; reuse fully permitted with attribution | **None** |

**Bottom line:** of the six families, three (Lexikos/eLex, LREC-COLING, JOHD/Cultural
Analytics) are CC-BY — **zero rights risk, author keeps copyright**. One (De Gruyter
Lexicographica) is effectively **in-house** with the book's own primary publisher. Two need
a CTA read *at acceptance*: **OUP (IJL/DSH)** and **Nodus (BGS)** — and only if the article
publishes *before* the corresponding chapter ships.

## Action rule (the only standing task this table generates)

> **When any of the 15 articles is accepted at a journal, before its chapter ships:**
> read that venue's Copyright Transfer / Licence-to-Publish agreement, confirm the
> author-self-reuse-in-monograph clause, and record the exact clause + date in this table's
> row. For the CC-BY venues (🟢) this is automatic (attribution only). For the OUP and Nodus
> rows (🟡) it is a two-minute CTA read, not a permission request.

Because publication and the book run in parallel, this is a rolling check, not a one-time
gate — and for any article that publishes *after* the book, it never triggers at all.
